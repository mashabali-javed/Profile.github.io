About Me

Hi, I’m Mashab Ali Javed, an engineer-turned-researcher working at the intersection of machine learning, data privacy and cybersecurity. With a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from National University of Sciences & Technology (NUST) and a Master’s in Electrical Engineering from Sir Syed CASE Institute of Technology (CGPA 3.83/4.0), my academic path has been defined by a passion for science and technology. My research focuses on privacy-preserving AI and federated learning—techniques that enable models to learn from data without requiring it to leave its source. My master's thesis focused on the application of privacy preserving federated learning in medical diagnosis, where I achieved diagnostic accuracy comparable to existing classification algorithms while significantly enhancing patient data privacy. Driven by a passion for secure and ethical innovation, I aim to contribute to research that not only advances the state of technology but also respects user rights and global data protection standards. I'm particularly interested in designing systems that are privacy-aware by design and practical in real-world, decentralized settings.
